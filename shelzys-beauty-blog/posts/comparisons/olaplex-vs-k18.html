Olaplex No. 3 vs K18: The Definitive Bond-Repair Showdown

By Shelzy | 10 min read | Updated February 2026
Disclosure: This post contains affiliate links. If you purchase through these links, I may earn a small commission at no extra cost to you. See my full disclosure.

Olaplex and K18 are the two biggest names in bond repair, but they work completely differently. I tested both on my bleach-damaged hair for 8 weeks — using Olaplex on one side and K18 on the other (yes, really) — to give you a definitive comparison.

How They Work

Olaplex No. 3 ($30) uses bis-aminopropyl diglycol dimaleate to relink broken disulfide bonds. It's a pre-shampoo treatment — apply to damp hair, leave for 10+ minutes, then shampoo and condition normally.

K18 Leave-In Mask ($29) uses a patented bioactive peptide (K18PEPTIDE) that reconnects broken keratin chains. It's a leave-in — apply to damp, freshly-shampooed hair and let it air dry. No rinsing, no conditioner needed.

My 8-Week Results

Weeks 1-2: K18 showed faster initial improvement. Hair felt noticeably smoother and more elastic after the very first use. Olaplex was subtle.

Weeks 3-4: Olaplex started catching up. Both sides showed significant improvement in strength and reduced breakage.

Weeks 5-8: By week 8, both treatments delivered comparable results in overall hair health. K18 had a slight edge in smoothness; Olaplex had a slight edge in natural movement.

Final Verdict

Choose K18 if: You want faster results, prefer leave-in convenience, and don't mind the higher per-use cost.

Choose Olaplex No. 3 if: You want the original proven formula, prefer a more affordable per-use cost, and don't mind the extra time.

My honest pick: K18 by a slight margin, purely for the convenience factor. Four minutes with no rinsing changed my wash routine.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I use both Olaplex and K18?

Yes, but on different wash days. They work on different types of bonds and can complement each other.

How often should I use each?

Olaplex: once a week. K18: every wash for the first 4-6 washes, then as needed.

Do these work on virgin (undamaged) hair?

Yes, but the effects are less dramatic since there's less damage to repair.
