## üè† HOME OFFICE DEDUCTION

### Simplified Method (Easier!)
- $5 per square foot
- Maximum 300 sq ft = $1,500 max deduction
- No complex calculations needed

### Regular Method (Potentially More)
- Calculate % of home used for business
- Deduct that % of: rent/mortgage interest, utilities, insurance, repairs
- Keep all home expense receipts

### Requirements
‚úÖ Space used **regularly** for business
‚úÖ Space used **exclusively** for business
‚úÖ Principal place of business OR where you meet clients

## üöó VEHICLE DEDUCTION

### Standard Mileage Rate (2025)
**67 cents per mile** for business use

### What to Track
- Date of trip
- Starting & ending location
- Business purpose
- Total miles driven

### What Qualifies
‚úÖ Trips to post office for shipping
‚úÖ Trips to buy supplies
‚úÖ Trips to meet clients
‚úÖ Trips to pick up inventory
‚ùå Commuting to regular job (NOT deductible)
‚ùå Personal errands (NOT deductible)

## üíª EQUIPMENT DEDUCTION

### Section 179 Deduction
Deduct the **full cost** in the year purchased (up to limits):
- Computer/laptop
- Camera/photography equipment
- Printer/scanner
- Craft equipment (Cricut, sewing machine, etc.)

### 2025 Limit: $1,160,000

## üì¶ COST OF GOODS SOLD (COGS)

For product sellers, track separately:
- Raw materials/supplies to make products
- Inventory purchased for resale
- Shipping TO you (inbound freight)

**This goes in Part III of Schedule C, NOT as expenses**

## ‚ö†Ô∏è COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID

1. **Missing receipts** - No receipt = no deduction
2. **Mixing personal/business** - Keep separate accounts
3. **Forgetting mileage** - Track EVERY business trip
4. **Not tracking small expenses** - They add up!
5. **Over-claiming home office** - Must be exclusive use
6. **Missing quarterly payments** - Can result in penalties
