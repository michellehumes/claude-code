## What to Look for in a Setting Spray

Not all setting sprays work the same way. Here's what separates the good from the great:

- **Hold level**: Some lock makeup in place, while others primarily add dewiness
- **Finish type**: Matte, dewy, or natural — choose based on your skin type
- **Transfer resistance**: The spray should prevent makeup from rubbing off on clothes and phones
- **Comfort**: It shouldn't feel tight, sticky, or heavy on skin

<Callout type="tip" title="Pro Tip">
Hold the bottle 8-10 inches from your face and spray in an X or T pattern for even coverage. Let it dry naturally — don't fan or blot.
</Callout>

## Setting Spray vs. Setting Powder: When to Use Each

Both extend wear, but they serve different purposes:

- **Setting spray**: Best for locking all layers together, adding hydration, and preventing transfer
- **Setting powder**: Best for absorbing oil, reducing shine, and preventing creasing
- **Both together**: The ultimate combo for all-day wear in any conditions

<Callout type="expert" title="Expert Advice">
For maximum staying power, use this layering technique: primer, foundation, setting powder in oily areas, then setting spray over everything. This sandwich method gives you the longest wear time possible.
</Callout>

## Tips for Different Skin Types

- **Oily skin**: Choose a matte-finish spray and pair with setting powder in the T-zone
- **Dry skin**: Look for hydrating sprays that add dewiness without drying you out
- **Combination skin**: Use powder only on oily areas, then spritz a natural-finish spray all over
- **Sensitive skin**: Avoid formulas with alcohol at the top of the ingredient list
