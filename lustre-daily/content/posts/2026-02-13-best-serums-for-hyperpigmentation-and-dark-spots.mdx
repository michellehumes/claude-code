## Understanding Hyperpigmentation

Before choosing a serum, it helps to understand what you're treating:

- **Post-inflammatory hyperpigmentation (PIH)**: Dark marks left after acne or injury
- **Sun spots**: Brown patches caused by UV exposure over time
- **Melasma**: Hormonal pigmentation that appears as larger patches
- **Uneven skin tone**: General dullness and lack of radiance

<Callout type="info">
Different types of hyperpigmentation respond to different ingredients. PIH often fades faster than melasma, which may require a combination approach.
</Callout>

## How to Build a Hyperpigmentation Routine

The most effective approach combines multiple strategies:

1. **Morning**: Vitamin C serum + moisturizer + SPF 30 or higher
2. **Evening**: Gentle cleanser + treatment serum (niacinamide or retinoid) + moisturizer
3. **Weekly**: Chemical exfoliant (AHA/BHA) to boost cell turnover
4. **Always**: Consistent sun protection is non-negotiable

<Callout type="expert" title="Expert Advice">
Patience is key with hyperpigmentation treatments. Most serums need 8-12 weeks of consistent use before you see significant fading. Take progress photos monthly to track changes.
</Callout>

## Key Ingredients to Look For

- **Vitamin C (L-ascorbic acid)**: Inhibits melanin production and brightens
- **Niacinamide**: Prevents pigment transfer to skin cells
- **Alpha arbutin**: Gentle alternative to hydroquinone
- **Azelaic acid**: Targets melanin production and reduces inflammation
- **Tranexamic acid**: Emerging ingredient showing strong results for melasma

## The Bottom Line

Fading hyperpigmentation takes time and consistency, but the right serums can make a genuine difference. Start with a vitamin C serum in the morning and a niacinamide or gentle retinoid at night, and always protect your progress with daily SPF.
